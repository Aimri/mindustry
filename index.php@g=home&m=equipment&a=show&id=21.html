Overview Specifications Features Cases

Overview:

Lead alloy anodes are permanently installed in the electrolytic cell to form electrode pairs with cathodes. The cells are electrocally connected in series and a voltage is aplied across the anode-cathode pair to produce Copper on cathode by electro-winning. During this process, oxygen is evolved at the anode surface.

Loaded cathodes are periodically removed from the electro-winning electrolytic cells by a lifting bale and overhead crane for stripping cathode. Blank cathodes are then returned to electro-winning electrolytic cells with the lifting bale and the process is repeated.

Features:

Pb and Ca and Sn will be added into the melting furnace to produce the lead alloy anode liquid as required. After mixing well enough and ready for discharging to cast, the lead alloy liquid will be sampled and analysis.                                                                                                                     
A final quality control inspection shall be undertaken on each anode plate before packing to check compliance with dimensions, tolerances and general qauality of the assembly.
 A hanging test shall be conducted on all completed assemblies. The off plumb measurement at the bottom edge of the plate anode must not exceed 3mm, when the plate is supported on the underside of each end of hanger bar.


An electrical conduction performance check.


As above measures, the anode has below advantages:

  1. High mechanical strength against distortion and creep (form stability);
  2. Low oxygen over potential for oxygen evolution;
  3. Quick and stable formation of hard, compact and adherent protective corrosion layer (conditioning);
